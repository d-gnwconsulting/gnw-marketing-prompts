# Competitor Gap for AI

**Category:** GEO
**Model tested:** Claude Sonnet 4.6
**Last updated:** 2026-05-13

## What it does

Identifies topics, questions, and content angles that AI systems associate with your competitors but not with you. Reveals where your content strategy has gaps that are costing you AI citations.

## Input needed

- Your brand name and description
- 2-4 competitor names
- Your industry/niche

## Prompt

```
You are a GEO competitive analyst. Based on your training data, help me understand the content gaps between my brand and my competitors in terms of AI search visibility.

My brand: [your brand name]
What we do: [1-2 sentence description]
Competitors: [list 2-4 competitor names]
Industry: [your industry/niche]

Analyze and answer:

1. **Topics competitors are known for** — What subjects, questions, or content areas are your competitors commonly associated with in AI responses? List the top 5 per competitor.

2. **Topics I'm likely missing** — Based on competitor coverage and my description, what content topics am I probably not covering that AI systems would cite me for?

3. **Quick wins** — Which 3 missing topics would be fastest to create content for and have the highest GEO impact?

4. **Content format recommendations** — For each quick win, what content format (FAQ, how-to guide, comparison page, definition page) would be most citable by AI?

Note: Be explicit about the limits of your knowledge — if you don't have reliable data on a specific brand, say so.
```

## Example

**Input:**
- My brand: [a mid-size paid media agency]
- What we do: Manage Google Ads and Meta Ads campaigns for e-commerce brands
- Competitors: [Agency A], [Agency B], [Agency C]
- Industry: Paid media / performance marketing

**Output:**

**1. Topics competitors are commonly associated with in AI responses:**

[Agency A]: ROAS benchmarks for e-commerce, Google Performance Max strategy, scaling Meta ad spend, creative testing frameworks
[Agency B]: First-party data strategy, cookieless advertising, Meta Advantage+ campaigns, paid social for DTC brands
[Agency C]: Google Shopping feed optimization, Attribution modeling, agency pricing models, e-commerce ad budgeting

**2. Topics you're likely missing:**

- ROAS benchmarks by e-commerce category (high-volume query, almost no agency owns it)
- How to audit a paid media agency (users ask AI this before hiring — answer it and you appear in that moment)
- Meta Advantage+ vs manual campaigns: when to use each
- Google Performance Max explained for e-commerce brands
- How to set a paid media budget for an e-commerce brand
- Attribution models for multi-channel e-commerce campaigns

**3. Quick wins (fastest to create, highest GEO impact):**

1. **"How to audit a paid media agency"** — High-intent query, users ask this at the exact moment they're considering hiring. A thorough guide positions you as the trustworthy answer.
2. **"ROAS benchmarks for e-commerce by category"** — Data-driven content AI loves to cite. Include a simple table with realistic ranges by product category.
3. **"Google Performance Max for e-commerce brands: a plain-language guide"** — Competitors have thin coverage; a complete, current guide fills the gap immediately.

**4. Recommended content formats:**

1. Audit guide → **How-to guide** with a numbered checklist (AI extracts lists easily)
2. ROAS benchmarks → **Data page** with a table (tables are highly citable)
3. Performance Max guide → **Definition page + FAQ** (definition + Q&A is the most-cited format in AI responses)

---

## When to use this

Run this prompt before building a new content calendar. The gaps it identifies should inform your first batch of GEO-optimized content.
